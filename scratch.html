Introduction

Before this semester, I was always afraid of taking any information system class because I had never done any coding. Although I wish I was able to avoid any of it, this IS 201 class has taught me many skills that I believe will be useful in my future career.

Here are the three things I liked the most about the class.

    • Visual Basic Analysis (VBA)
    • Tableau
    • Hypertext Markup Language (HTML)


Visual Basic Analysis (VBA)

I remember Professor Wells saying VBA is the hardest part of this course. Honestly, this wasn't the case for me as I really enjoyed coding with VBA. Through this module, I came to understand the importance of automating calculations in Excel specially when there is a lot of information to be processed. I thought if I were to major in information system, I would like to specialize in VBA (if there was a career path for that).


Tableau

Although the module for Tableau was relatively short compared to other modules, I enjoyed working with graphs and extracting information from a data source. I would say that, in part, I liked Tableau because it was the easiest for me in this course. However, that does not mean I took it lightly. This Tableau visualization skills could definitely be useful when I am trying to look al a specific information from a data base.

Hypertext Markup Language (HTML)

This was my biggest fear. The reason I did not want to take an information system class was because I wanted to avoid this type of coding. However, as I followed through the videos on MyEducator and did some practice on my own, I came to understand that even coding, something I thought was really difficult, became easier than expected. Now, even though it takes time, I enjoy coding with HTML, and like VBA I would take courses focused on HTML or web development if I were to major in information system.
